SEO Basics: Where Every Ranking Actually Starts

Before writing a single line of content, every SEO project starts in the same place: keyword research. This is the process of finding out exactly what words people type into Google when they're looking for something related to your business.

I use Google Keyword Planner for this — it shows search volume (how many people search a term) and competition level (how hard it is to rank for it). The goal isn't to chase the highest-volume keyword; it's to find keywords with real search demand and realistic competition for where a site currently stands.

Once keywords are identified, they guide everything downstream: the content you write, the page titles, the headers, and even the internal links between pages. Skipping this step is the most common beginner mistake in SEO — writing content first and hoping it ranks, instead of researching first and writing with intent.
